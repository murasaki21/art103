The Story

In a world where giant robot battles against various evil organizations trying to take over the world every week in urban cities, buildings are often damaged and destroyed as collateral damage. Despite the destruction, the battles are quite the spectacle with lasers shooting from mechas and sparks flying everywhere. Hence, the urban battles continue. However, rebuilding destroyed cities only for them to be destroyed again the next week is tedious and exhausts our natural resources. While the threat of evil isn’t going away anytime soon, the tediousness of rebuilding a city can be reduced. Thankfully, architecture firm ConneXt Design developed a two-piece puzzle system named +e+rix (pronounced tetrix) that can create building infrastructures efficientlywithout needing traditional bonders such as glue or nails. The pieces consists of Xs and +s and with their patented material Tetromium, the broken pieces can be reused through melting down and re-casting the material, so waste won’t be an issue.

Artist Statement

Overall, the assignment was pretty simple and it was fun experimenting with different materials and render settings. Starting with the demo teapot, the tutorial was pretty easy to follow. I didn't really have any challenges making the teapot, but I was slighlty annoyed that whenever I wanted to create a new sketch, after I clicked on the plane desired for the sketch, Fusion would automatically flip the user orientaion, so I would constantly need fix the orientation with the cube in the upper right corner. Moving onto the personal teapot, I based the sketch off of my narwhal mug, but changed the horn into a pouring spout and added a blowhole lid. In terms of challenges, I couldn't figure out how to create the tail at the end of the handle, leading to me removing the tailfin in the final model. Fusion would keep giving me error messages that the lofts would intersect with the main body of the teapot even though I drew in rails and set the loft as new body.
