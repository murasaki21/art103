Artist Statement

New Tung Kee Noodles and TK Noodles are resturants with very similar names, interior design, menu, vibes, and logos. To the point most people would mistaken them as the same company. Despite their similarities and the potential for the original TK Noodles to sue New Tung Kee, they decided not to sue and allow both companies to grow and develop on their own accord. The two companies existing simutaneously in harmony inspired me to use the Yin Yang Symbol for both my stamp and sign.

Starting with the stamp, I was trying to figure out how to convey that New Tung Kee and TK Noodles are very similar yet different. While a mirror or a coin could convey this message, it was hard to figure out how it could be translated through an ink print. Then I shifted my focus from copying/reflection towards living harmoniously which led to using the Yin Yang Symbol. On a side note, the restaurants sell Chinese-styled noodles and dishes so it was also fitting to use the Chinese symbol of harmony to convey my message. In terms of difficulties, the only major one is creating enough negative space for the bit to cut in between while retaining enough information. I had to simplify both logos quite a bit for the bit to cut between the circle and the logo which took around 9 iterations. One mistake I made when creating the stamp was not creating a deep enough cut. While it was fine for the inking process, I didn't extrude properly in Fusion, leading to a shallower cut. In terms of sucesses, by making most of my stamp elements round, I didn't need to deal with the router bit not getting into sharp corners.

With the sign, I took my original design for my stamp which included the more intricate logos in the circles and exported it into Fusion. I started with a rectangle sketch, then used the circle tool to cut the rectanble, extruded the circle slightly, imported the sign SVG, extruded the SVG, then applied high glossy paint textures to the sign. Compared to the stamp, creating the sign was pretty straightfoward since I already had the base for the sign done. Compositing the sign into photoshop was pretty simple once I got the sign rendered in the right angle (took 3 tries, but it's faster than trying to create negative space for the CNC cut).
